Methodology

ARES Gridiron Market has two scoring systems: ARES Score and Market Score.

ARES Score

ARES Score measures on-field football quality. It is not the same as Market Score.

Market Score

Market Score measures football asset value. A younger player can have a higher Market Score than his current ARES Score because of age, draft value, position scarcity, or upside.

A player can be great on the field but have a lower Market Score because of age, durability, limited upside, or market demand.

At launch, pros and college players are ranked. CFL, UFL, FCS, JUCO, high school, and transfer portal players are tracked only. Tracked-only players are not ranked against NFL or college players because data quality is thinner.

High school players are tracked only, not ranked against pro or college players. Public high school data quality varies widely, and several commercial recruiting and video platforms restrict automated collection. ARES Gridiron uses manual entries, licensed/user-provided files, approved public school/state pages, and source reference links. Commercial recruiting/profile links may be stored for reference, but their protected content is not bulk scraped.

The local NFL reference workspace includes position-specific ARES scripts with soft-100 scoring, empirical-Bayes volume shrinkage, opponent adjustment, EPA-based efficiency features, and position-specific composites. Those files stay private and are used only as source reference for later pipeline steps.

ARES Score Formula

ARES Score = 0.30 * Performance + 0.20 * Efficiency + 0.15 * Role / Usage + 0.15 * Opponent Adjustment + 0.10 * Volume + 0.05 * Durability + 0.05 * Trend

Market Score Formula

Market Score = 0.25 * ARES Score + 0.20 * Age / Upside + 0.20 * Position Value + 0.15 * League Tier + 0.10 * Market Signal + 0.05 * Movement Value + 0.05 * Durability

High School Tracking Score

This watchlist score is separate from ARES Score and is not used to rank high school players against college or pro players.

High School Tracking Score = 0.35 * Recruiting Signal + 0.25 * Offer Strength + 0.15 * Position Scarcity + 0.10 * Production Signal + 0.10 * Film / Profile Verification + 0.05 * Movement Momentum

Scores and Movement Data

ARES Gridiron uses approved APIs, licensed feeds, manual score files, coach/school submissions, and allowlisted school/state sources for score and movement data. GitHub Pages is static, so feeds are refreshed on a schedule and exported as JSON. Commercial platforms that restrict scraping are not bulk collected. High school data is treated with source confidence because coverage varies by state, school, and reporting system.

High School Data

High school score data can come from approved feeds such as ScoreStream partner access, school/state association sources, and manual coach/school submissions. Commercial recruiting, video, and stat platforms may be stored as reference links when manually provided, but ARES does not bulk scrape protected data. High school players and scores receive confidence grades.
